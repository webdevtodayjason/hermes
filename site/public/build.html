Assembly

  • 1. Flash the firmware — from your browser on the FLASH page (WebSerial, Chrome/Edge), or with PlatformIO from the repo
  • 2. Load the SD card — copy the hermes-buddy/ asset pack (face animations, sounds) to a FAT32 card, insert
  • 3. Plug a speaker — the kit's included speaker goes straight into the board's speaker jack; set the on-board volume dial to taste
  • 4. Battery (optional) — the LiPo plugs into the BAT connector and tucks under the board; four brass standoffs give it clearance inside the case
  • 5. Case — snap-fit print, M2.5 bolts on the bottom
  • 6. USB into your gateway machine — the plugin finds her automatically on any port
  • 7. Install the plugin — one command, see PLUGIN; Wi-Fi credentials are provisioned over USB, no SD editing

Orientation: she sits landscape, USB exiting to the side. If the screen comes up inverted for your stand, rotation is a one-line config over USB — never a reflash.

The board, briefly

ESP32-S3 (240 MHz dual-core, 8 MB flash), 2.8" IPS at 240×320 driven through LovyanGFX, CST328 capacitive touch, I2S mono amplifier to the speaker jack, SD over SDMMC, native USB-CDC serial. The familiar uses USB as its control channel and Wi-Fi only to pull audio — so it works fully even on a network-less bench, just silently.
